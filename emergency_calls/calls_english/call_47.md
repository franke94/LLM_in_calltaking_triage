Emergency Call 47

calltaker: Fire and Rescue Service emergency line, in which location is the emergency?

caller: Uh… here in [City], in the logistics centre, in the staff canteen. I don’t know the exact address off the top of my head, but it’s the canteen on the premises.

calltaker: What is the name of the company?

caller: It’s the TransLog GmbH logistics centre… that’s written everywhere here.

calltaker: What is your name?

caller: I’m Laura Meier.

calltaker: Ms. Meier, what happened?

caller: A colleague has, while eating, a piece of steak somehow stuck in his throat… he’s gagging all the time and cannot swallow, saliva is running out of his mouth. He says it feels like pressure behind his sternum.

calltaker: Is he conscious?

caller: Yes, yes, he’s awake, he’s looking at me and responding… but totally panicked and can only speak in short, broken sentences.

calltaker: Is he currently getting air?

caller: Yes, he’s getting air, he’s breathing… he also says he does not have shortness of breath. He coughs and gags repeatedly, but nothing is coming up.

calltaker: But he can still breathe?

caller: Yes, he can still breathe, definitely. It sounds normal, only this gagging in between, and he keeps drooling saliva.

calltaker: Is he cyanotic or does he have any abnormal facial colour?

caller: No, he’s not blue. He’s rather pale and sweating, but his lips are normal, not blue.

calltaker: Any other pain?

caller: He keeps pointing to his chest, centrally behind the sternum, and says it presses and hurts there. Otherwise… nothing, I think, no belly pain.

calltaker: I’ll send an ambulance. Please stay with your colleague. You can try delivering back blows between the shoulder blades to dislodge the piece of steak.

caller: Okay… the company first aider is already here and said we should not give him anything to drink. Back blows… should I do that now even though he’s still breathing? He’s sitting bent forward and just gagging.

calltaker: Yes, that can help; you may support him with that.

caller: Okay, I’ll do it now… [short pause] I’ve just slapped him a few times between the shoulder blades, but only saliva came out, no piece of meat. He keeps gagging and is becoming very agitated.

calltaker: But he’s still getting air okay?

caller: Yes, he’s still getting air, he can also briefly say “yes”… but he cannot swallow at all, he’s just drooling.

calltaker: Okay. The ambulance is on its way. If his breathing worsens the company first aider should attempt the Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts), okay?

caller: Yes, okay, I’ll tell him that right away. Please hurry, it looks really bad… he’s panicking and gagging constantly.

calltaker: The crew are on their way. If anything deteriorates call this number again, okay?

caller: Yes, I will. We’ll stay with him and make sure he doesn’t drink anything. Thank you.
