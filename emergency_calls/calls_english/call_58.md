Emergency Call 58

Dialog

- calltaker: Emergency services, fire brigade and ambulance. In which town is the emergency?

- caller: Uh, we are in Munich, in our company's data center, in the technical corridor… I don't know the street right now, this is a secure area.

- calltaker: What is the name of the company?

- caller: It's at DataCore Systems GmbH… our data center here.

- calltaker: What happened?

- caller: A colleague, about in his mid‑fifties, suddenly stopped, put his hand on his chest and said briefly that it felt tight and his heart was fluttering… and then he just collapsed and is now lying on the floor.

- calltaker: Is he conscious now?

- caller: No, he's not responding… we called to him and shook his shoulders, nothing.

- calltaker: Is he lying on his back?

- caller: Yes, he's on his back in the corridor, we put him like that so he could get air… but it's really cramped here with cables.

- calltaker: Okay, look in his mouth.

- caller: Uh yes, one moment… I'm opening his mouth… there's nothing in it, no food or anything, just saliva. I don't see anything blocking it.

- calltaker: Okay, tilt his head back slightly and tell me if he is breathing.

- caller: Okay… head back… I'm putting my ear to him… uh… I can't really hear anything. His chest is barely moving… maybe some very odd gasping, very irregular, but not normal breathing.

- calltaker: Okay, the paramedics are already on their way. You must start cardiopulmonary resuscitation now.

- caller: Oh God… okay, yes, tell me how! We also have one of those defibrillator things, the security officer is getting it right now.

- calltaker: Great. Tell me when the defibrillator arrives. You need to expose his chest if possible. Then find approximately the centre of the chest, roughly between the nipples.

- caller: Okay, I'm opening the jacket and lifting the shirt… yes, chest is exposed. My hands are now in the middle… should I press, yes?

- calltaker: Exactly. Now compress 5–6 cm with straight arms into the chest and then allow full recoil.

- caller: Okay, I'm pressing… one, two, three… oh man… I'm pressing as hard as I can, arms straight… one, two, three, four… he doesn't move at all.

- calltaker: Okay, keep doing that until the defibrillator arrives. I will count the rhythm for you…

- caller: Okay… please count, otherwise I'll get confused… it's so loud here… I'll keep pressing, don't stop…
