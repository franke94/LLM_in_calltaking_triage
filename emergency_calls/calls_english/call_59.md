Emergency call — Fire and Rescue Service. In which town is the emergency?

caller: In Dortmund, at a municipal depot, in the storage hall… I’m not sure if that’s a precise address.

calltaker: Can you give me the street name?

caller: Um… I’m not standing at the sign… it’s the Westfalenhütte depot, we came in through a large gate. I can send someone out to read the street name at the entrance!

calltaker: Great. What happened?

caller: A forklift collided with an open roller door while maneuvering and then tipped over… a pallet with metal parts slid off. Two colleagues were hit, one is trapped!

calltaker: Okay. Is the colleague still trapped now?

caller: Yes, he is still trapped, sort of to the side… the forklift is lying on its side and the metal profiles are on top of him. We can’t get to him easily, and one guy here wants to pull him out, but I don’t think that’s a good idea…

calltaker: No, it’s best to leave him where he is until we know more. I have already alerted the rescue services. Is the trapped colleague still responsive?

caller: He was briefly awake a moment ago, but now he’s very groggy; his eyes are only half open… he’s just mumbling. He barely responds to questions.

calltaker: Where is he trapped, which body part?

caller: In the pelvic/leg area, I think… his hip and legs are somehow under the metal parts, he’s lying at an angle. His upper body is free, but he can’t get out.

calltaker: Okay. Is he breathing normally?

caller: He’s still breathing, yes… but not normally — rather shallow and kind of fast, like he’s gasping for air. You can hear it, and he’s getting paler.

calltaker: Do you see any injuries? Is there bleeding anywhere?

caller: Yes, he has a scalp laceration on the head, there was a lot of blood at first; we’re applying direct pressure with a cloth. There’s also a laceration on the arm, but it’s less severe… the head was the worst.

calltaker: All right. It’s good that you’re holding pressure there.

caller: Okay… we’ll keep applying pressure. Can you tell me how long it will be, he’s really acting strange… there’s so much chaos, everyone’s talking over each other.

calltaker: The crews will be with you shortly. Is the other colleague injured as well?

caller: Yes, the other is a female colleague, early 30s… she was hit, we pulled her out of the hazard area. She’s conscious but has severe pain and is holding her chest/shoulder, and she’s shaking a lot. I don’t think she has major bleeding.

calltaker: Is she responsive?

caller: Yes, she is responsive; she keeps saying “that hurts” and asking for water. She looks really pale and in shock.

calltaker: Is she breathing normally?

caller: She’s breathing, but also rapid… as if she can’t breathe deeply because of the pain. She can speak but only briefly because it hurts.

calltaker: Okay, the crews will arrive in about a minute.

caller: Okay… thank you. I’ll actually send someone to the gate now to give you the street name and direct them, okay? And we’ll stay with both of them.

calltaker: Exactly.

caller: All right… I’ll tell them right away. Please hurry, the trapped person is getting very quiet…
