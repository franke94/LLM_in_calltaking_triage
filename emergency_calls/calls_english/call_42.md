- calltaker: "Emergency call for fire department and ambulance service. In which town is the emergency?"

- caller: "In Cologne, in Ehrenfeld… please, it’s about my husband, he’s suddenly talking very strangely."

- calltaker: "Which street are you on?"

- caller: "Um… Venloer Street, number 214… second floor, but there is an elevator."

- calltaker: "What is your name?"

- caller: "My name is Sandra Meier… please send someone quickly."

- calltaker: "Is your husband also Meier?"

- caller: "Yes, exactly, Thomas Meier… he is 62."

- calltaker: "Is your husband conscious now?"

- caller: "Yes, yes, he is awake… he’s looking at me as well, but acting strange, as if he can’t find the right words."

- calltaker: "But he is responding normally, so he is not drowsy?"

- caller: "Not really drowsy, no… more unsettled. He answers, but slowly and sometimes only a wrong word comes out."

- calltaker: "He doesn’t have any breathing problems, does he?"

- caller: "No, he is getting air, he is breathing normally… not gasping or anything."

- calltaker: "Has he ever had anything like a stroke before?"

- caller: "Not a major one… but a few years ago he had a short episode; he was dizzy and the doctor said it could have been a precursor."

- calltaker: "Does he have other pre-existing conditions?"

- caller: "Yes, he has high blood pressure and high cholesterol… and palpitations — the doctor once said atrial fibrillation."

- calltaker: "Ms. Meier, the crew are already on their way to you. Your husband should remain seated somewhere comfortable and stay calm, and should not eat or drink."

- caller: "Okay, yes, I’ll sit him on the sofa… Thomas, stay seated, okay? He was just about to have something to drink, but I told him not to."

- calltaker: "If anything changes, call again. The crew are on their way."

- caller: "Yes… please hurry. I’ll stay with him and call immediately if anything gets worse."
