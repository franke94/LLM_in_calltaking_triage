Dialogue

- calltaker: Emergency services, fire department and ambulance, in which location is the emergency?

- caller: In Cologne, in a ceramics studio in an industrial area… um, I don’t know the exact street off the top of my head, but I can read it off the sign on the door in a moment.

- calltaker: What is the company name?

- caller: It’s “Ton & Glas Ceramic Studio”… that’s written on the sign at the entrance.

- calltaker: What is the street?

- caller: One moment… I’ll go to the door… uh… “Industriestraße 18,” in Cologne.

- calltaker: Understood. What happened?

- caller: A colleague got something splashed into his right eye while glazing — a strongly alkaline glaze, it’s labeled “pH over 12.” He’s screaming, holding his eye and rubbing it like crazy, it’s tearing a lot.

- calltaker: Is any of the solution still present such that others could be injured?

- caller: Yes, something was knocked over on the table, the floor is wet and slippery, and the open containers are still there. We’re standing back a bit, but it’s all still open, yes.

- calltaker: Okay, then make sure nobody walks into it and ideally leave the room.

- caller: Okay, yes — we’ll tell everyone. But the colleague… he’s sitting right at the sink; we’re rinsing his eye with water now. Should we keep him in there briefly for that?

- calltaker: If you are safe there, yes. Continue to irrigate the eye.

- caller: Yes, we’re at the sink, a bit away from the chemical, I’m watching to make sure nobody steps in the puddle. We’re continuing to rinse, but he’s thrashing about and says it burns like hell.

- calltaker: Is the colleague awake and responsive?

- caller: Yes, he’s awake, he’s screaming and answering too, but totally panicked. He says he only sees blurred in the right eye and can hardly open it because it hurts so much.

- calltaker: Any breathing problems?

- caller: No, not with breathing, he’s breathing normally, not coughing or anything. It’s really “only” the eye, but he’s in very severe pain.

- calltaker: The team is already on their way to you. You can continue to irrigate the eye; they’ll be there shortly.

- caller: Okay, we’ll keep rinsing, yes. Should I have the label or a safety data sheet ready for them? We have something here from the product.

- calltaker: Exactly. And if anything changes in his condition, call 112 again!

- caller: Understood, I will. We’ll keep irrigating and I’ll get the data sheet ready. If he gets any worse, I’ll call immediately.
