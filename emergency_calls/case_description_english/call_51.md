Current incident:
- In an indoor municipal swimming pool, a 34-year-old man suddenly developed severe breathing difficulty in the pool during a breath-hold (apnea) training session immediately after surfacing. Shortly thereafter he had marked coughing, frothy pink sputum, increasing cyanosis and exhaustion.
- Noticed by the lifeguard: patient is clinging to the pool edge, can only speak single words, appears panicked.
- Time course: onset immediately after surfacing, rapid progression within 5–10 minutes; patient was pulled to the pool edge and then brought to the first-aid area. Oxygen administration via the available emergency kit was unsuccessful/insufficient.

Situation on site:
- Environment: swimming pool hall, humid-warm air, open to the public.
- Persons involved: patient, 1 training partner, 2 lifeguards, several witnesses.
- Hazards/special features: risk of slipping, noisy environment, limited privacy; aspiration of water cannot be safely excluded.
- Behavior: lifeguards cooperative, performing first aid; training partner very agitated, reports "breathing technique training", attempts to explain and intermittently obstructs access.

Patient history:
- Preexisting conditions: none known; non-smoker according to companion.
- Medications: none known.
- Risks: repeated apnea/hyperventilation prior to the dive; possible swimming-induced pulmonary edema (SIPE) or post-immersion pulmonary edema; acute life-threatening respiratory insufficiency not excluded (NACA IV).
