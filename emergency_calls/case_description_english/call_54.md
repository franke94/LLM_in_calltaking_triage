Current event:
- What happened? In a municipal library, a 58-year-old man develops sudden-onset heart racing with dizziness and a retrosternal pressure sensation without radiation during a reading.
- How was the emergency noticed? Seat neighbors notice pallor and unsteadiness when he stood up; the patient sits down again, appears briefly “absent,” but remains responsive. Library staff called emergency services (112).
- Time course: Symptoms for approx. 20 minutes, worsening. No syncope, no seizure activity. Patient drank several espressos beforehand, ate little.

Situation on site:
- Environment: Event room of the library, quiet indoor premises, cramped rows of chairs.
- Number of people involved: Patient, approx. 25 audience members, 2 staff; 1 first aider (former nurse).
- Hazards/special features: Crowd of people, restricted access for a stretcher; AED available in the foyer.
- Behavior: Witnesses cooperative but unsettled; first aider measures pulse “very fast,” reassures the patient.

Patient history:
- Pre-existing conditions: Arterial hypertension, known paroxysmal atrial fibrillation, no known coronary artery disease.
- Medications: Metoprolol irregularly, Apixaban according to the patient “mostly.”
- Risks: Stress, caffeine, possibly inadequate anticoagulation; recent upper respiratory tract infection. (NACA III)
