**Current event:**  
- **What happened?** 34-year-old patient with acute, severe swallowing disorder (dysphagia) and retrosternal pressure after ingestion of beef ("steak stuck"). Drooling from the mouth; patient can neither drink nor swallow saliva.  
- **How was the emergency noticed?** Colleague noticed repeated gagging, coughing without effective expectoration, increasing restlessness. Patient speaks only in short phrases, denies shortness of breath.  
- **Timeline:** Onset about 25 minutes ago in the canteen. Initial attempt to drink → deterioration, then increased salivation. After unsuccessfully waiting, emergency call placed.

**Situation on site:**  
- **Environment:** Company canteen of a logistics center, seated at a table, food remnants and a water bottle in front of him.  
- **Number of people involved:** Patient, 4 colleagues, company paramedic.  
- **Hazards/particulars:** Risk of aspiration with further attempts to drink; patient is panicked but hemodynamically stable. No signs of allergic reaction (no hives/swelling).  
- **Behavior of witnesses/first responders:** Colleagues repeatedly offered water; company paramedic prevented this, calmed the patient, monitored respiration.

**Patient history:**  
- **Relevant pre-existing conditions:** Known eosinophilic esophagitis, recurrent food-impaction episodes ("food getting stuck"), previously resolved endoscopically.  
- **Medications:** Proton pump inhibitor as needed; inhaled corticosteroid used as swallowed therapy irregularly.  
- **Known risks:** Strictures/impaction, aspiration, dehydration due to lack of oral fluid intake. (NACA III)
