# A – Airway Assessment Guidelines
Context: Analysis of transcribed emergency calls

## Purpose
Assess airway patency and protection using caller descriptions.

## What to Evaluate
- ability to speak
- abnormal airway sounds
- obstruction risk
- swelling or choking

## Caller Language Indicators

### Normal / Likley Patent
- speaks normally
- answers clearly
- full sentences possible

### Possible Compromise
- hoarse voice
- difficulty speaking
- choking episode
- coughing after swallowing
- “only single words”

### Critical Indicators
- unable to speak
- gurgling sounds
- snoring sounds
- stridor / high-pitched breathing
- foreign body airway obstruction
- tongue or throat swelling

## Assessment Rules
- Speaking full sentences → airway likely patent
- Abnormal sounds → airway compromise possible
- Silence ≠ normal (may indicate obstruction)

## Red Flags
- choking
- stridor
- gurgling
- unable to speak
- rapidly worsening symptoms

## Severity Scale
0 = no information given or no conclusions possible
1 = no impairment suspected 
2 = significant impairment  
3 = critical airway compromise

## Missing Information (examples)
- Can the patient speak normally?
- Any choking event?
- Any swelling of mouth or throat?

## Output Reminder
Only use transcript evidence. Do not diagnose.
