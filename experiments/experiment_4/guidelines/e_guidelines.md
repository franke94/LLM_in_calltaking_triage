# E – Exposure & Environment Assessment Guidelines
Context: Emergency call transcript analysis

## Purpose
Assess injuries, trauma mechanisms, environmental hazards, and pain.

## What to Evaluate
- trauma mechanism
- visible injuries
- burns
- environmental hazards
- severe pain
- temperature exposure

## Caller Language Indicators

### Mechanism of Injury
- fall from height
- traffic accident
- explosion
- crushing injury

### Injuries / Conditions
- burns
- visible wounds
- deformities
- severe pain

### Environmental Hazards
- fire / smoke
- chemicals
- confined space
- extreme cold or heat

## Assessment Rules
- mechanism of injury can indicate hidden severity
- environmental hazards may endanger patient and responders
- pain is subjective but relevant
- information that are relevant for other ABCD categories should be documented in those sections (e.g., burns → airway, bleeding → circulation)

## Red Flags
- high-energy trauma
- hazardous environment
- severe burns
- multiple injuries

## Severity Scale
0 = no information given or no conclusions possible
1 = no impairment suspected
2 = significant trauma/environment risk  
3 = severe trauma or hazardous environment

## Missing Information
- What happened?
- Any visible injuries?
- Any fire, chemicals, or hazards?

## Output Reminder
Document mechanism and environment clearly.
