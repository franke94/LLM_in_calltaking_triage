# B – Breathing Assessment Guidelines
Context: Analysis of transcribed emergency calls

## Purpose
Assess breathing effectiveness and oxygenation.

## What to Evaluate
- breathing difficulty
- breathing effort and speed
- speech ability
- signs of oxygen deficiency

## Caller Language Indicators

### Normal
- breathing normal
- speaking normally
- no shortness of breath

### Mild / Moderate Impairment
- shortness of breath
- breathing fast
- cannot lie flat
- chest tightness
- wheezing

### Severe / Critical
- gasping for air
- cannot speak full sentences
- blue lips or face
- “gets no air”
- breathing stops
- extreme breathing effort

## Functional Indicators
- full sentences → mild/no distress
- single words → severe distress
- gasping → life-threatening

## Red Flags
- severe dyspnea
- cyanosis
- gasping
- breathing stops or nearly stops

## Severity Scale
0 = no information given or no conclusions possible
1 = no impairment suspected
2 = significant distress  
3 = life-threatening breathing problem

## Missing Information
- Is the patient breathing?
- Can they speak full sentences?
- Lip color normal?

## Output Reminder
Use functional descriptions, not numeric estimates unless stated.
