# C – Circulation Assessment Guidelines
Context: Analysis of emergency call transcripts

## Purpose
Assess perfusion, bleeding, and circulatory stability.

## What to Evaluate
- bleeding
- skin appearance
- collapse or fainting
- chest pain (symptom)
- signs of shock

## Caller Language Indicators

### Normal
- skin looks normal
- no bleeding
- patient responsive and stable

### Possible Circulatory Problem
- pale appearance
- sweating
- dizziness
- chest pain
- heart racing

### Critical Indicators
- heavy bleeding
- collapse or fainting
- cold, clammy skin
- gray or ashen skin
- signs of shock
- thoaracic pain with other symptoms (e.g., sweating, dizziness)

## Assessment Rules
- external bleeding has highest priority
- collapse suggests perfusion problem
- chest pain alone ≠ circulatory failure

## Red Flags
- uncontrolled bleeding
- collapse with ongoing symptoms
- signs of shock

## Severity Scale
0 = no information given or no conclusions possible
1 = no impairment suspected
2 = significant circulatory issue  
3 = life-threatening condition

## Missing Information
- Any bleeding?
- Skin pale, cold, sweaty?
- Did the patient collapse?

## Output Reminder
Do not infer blood pressure or pulse unless mentioned.
