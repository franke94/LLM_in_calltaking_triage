# E – Exposure/Externals Assessment 

## Context  
Analyze transcribed emergency calls to assess **exposure** and **external factors** (environment, trauma mechanism, temperature, chemicals, special incidents) and their impact on vital functions.

## Goal  
Assess whether external influences (trauma, temperature, chemicals, radiation, environment, special hazard situations, pain) pose no, moderate, or critical threat to the patient’s vital functions.  
“Other harmful influences affecting vital functions” and “pain” are grouped according to the international ABCDE nomenclature under **E – Exposure/Externals (Exposure/special incident situations)**; additionally, severe trauma mechanisms are considered, as described in accident analyses (e.g. GIDAS) as indicators of potentially severe injury.  

## Key Notes  
- Focus on **external influences** and **environmental factors**: trauma, fall height, traffic accident, heat/cold, fires/smoke, chemicals, radiation, entrapment, drowning/ice accidents, mass casualty incidents.  
- Also assess **pain severity** and its potential vital significance (e.g. extreme pain after trauma as an indicator of severe injury).  
- Severe trauma mechanisms (high speed, ejection from vehicle, entrapment, rollover, significant fall height) are considered potentially life-threatening even without a complete symptom description.  
- If information is incomplete, the **mechanism alone** (e.g. “severe car accident with entrapment”) may justify high urgency.  
- Uncertainty in the call (unclear situation, multiple affected persons, environmental danger) increases urgency and should be reflected in the confidence score.  

## Severity Scale  
| Severity | Description                                                                                 |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **0**    | No information available or no conclusion possible                                          |
| **1**    | Normal or minor impairment; no immediate intervention required                              |
| **2**    | Significant impairment; ambulance intervention required                                     |
| **3**    | Critical or life-threatening problem due to exposure/externals; requires ambulance + emergency physician |

## Example Indicators  

### Severity 1  
- No problems reported  
- Minor superficial injuries (e.g. small laceration, abrasion)  
- Mild pain without indication of severe injury  
- Brief, low-level exposure (e.g. minimal smoke, short cold/heat exposure) without symptoms  

### Severity 2  
- Minor to moderate accidents without clear signs of life-threatening injury  
- Hypothermia without impaired consciousness (shivering, cold, but responsive and oriented)  
- Hyperthermia or heat exhaustion without impaired consciousness  
- Significant thermal injuries (e.g. larger burns) in an awake and responsive patient  
- Effects of fire/smoke inhalation (coughing, irritation, discomfort) without acute vital threat  
- All injuries and bleeding where no immediate life-threatening condition is currently apparent  
- Moderate to severe pain in isolated extremity trauma without signs of shock  

### Severity 3  
- Severe traffic accident with occupant ejected from vehicle  
- Severe traffic accident with massive vehicle deformation and entrapment  
- Crush injury to torso or head  
- Fall from >3 meters with loss of consciousness or severe impairment  
- Explosion injury with significant force impact  
- Drowning/ice breakthrough accident with loss of consciousness or severe impairment  
- Massive hypothermia with unconsciousness or barely responsive patient  
- Mass casualty incident (MCI) with indication of many severely injured persons  
- Extremity injuries with marked deformity and/or neurological deficits and/or absent pulse in the affected limb  
- Extreme pain (e.g. after major trauma, burns, crush injury) with suspicion of severe injury or shock  
- Imminent delivery: regular contractions with signs of immediately impending birth; fetal head already visible  
- Delivery already occurred with signs of threat to mother or child: massive vaginal bleeding after birth; newborn with cyanosis, absent breathing, or limp body posture  

## Confidence Score  
- Range: **0.0–1.0**  
- High confidence = clear, explicit indicators in the transcript (e.g. mechanism + condition clearly described).  
- Low confidence = incomplete, indirect, or contradictory indicators.  
- Incorrect assessment with high confidence results in a high penalty.
