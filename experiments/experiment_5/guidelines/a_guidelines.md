# A – Airway Assessment Guidelines

**Context:** Analysis of transcribed emergency calls, quick assessment of airway symptoms.

## Purpose

Determine the airway status (patent, threatened, obstructed) based on caller descriptions. Breathing is _not_ part of this task.

## Evaluation Rules

- Focus solely on the airway, not on breathing or circulation.
- A clear airway is present if the patient is alert and speaking in full sentences.
- A threatened or obstructed airway may occur with    
    - Reduced consciousness
    - Stridor or abnormal airway sounds
    - Swelling in the mouth/throat
    - Obstruction with blood, saliva, vomit, foreign body, or food bolus

If transcript evidence is unclear, derive cautious conclusions (e.g. speaking = airway open). Reflect uncertainty in the confidence score.

## Severity Scale

0 = No information available  
1 = No or minor impairment, no intervention needed  
2 = Significant impairment, requires ambulance intervention  
3 = Critical or life-threatening airway compromise, needs ambulance + emergency physician

## Examples

**Severity 1:**

- No airway problem indicators
- Patient speaks clearly, no distress
- Mild irritation (e.g. cold) without vital risk

**Severity 2:**

- Mild airway restriction or minor injury
- Minor allergic reaction without swelling

**Severity 3:**

- Sudden slurred or “thick” speech, communication impossible
- Rapidly swelling airway
- Choking with ineffective breathing or inability to speak
- Severe bleeding in mouth/throat affecting breathing
- Airway/ventilation emergency in tracheostomized patient
- Reanimation 

## Confidence

Assign a score between 0.0–1.0.  
Low confidence = unclear or indirect evidence.  
High confidence = explicit transcript support.  
Incorrect high-confidence outputs result in high penalty.
