# C – Circulation Assessment 

## Context  
Analyze transcribed emergency calls to assess **circulation** (exclude airway and breathing).  

## Goal  
Identify whether circulation and cardiovascular status are normal, impaired, or critically compromised based on caller descriptions.  

## Key Notes  
- Assess only **circulation** (e.g. chest pain, pulse, blood pressure, dizziness, syncope, bleeding, signs of shock).  
- Typical circulation problems include arrhythmias, blood pressure crises, acute coronary syndromes, syncope, and relevant bleeding.  
- If evidence is unclear, infer cautiously (e.g. brief syncope with rapid recovery may still indicate at least moderate risk) and reflect uncertainty in the confidence score.  

## Severity Scale  
| Severity | Description                                                                            |
| -------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **0**    | No information available or no conclusion possible                                     |
| **1**    | Normal or minor impairment; no immediate intervention required                         |
| **2**    | Significant impairment; ambulance intervention required                                |
| **3**    | Critical or life-threatening circulatory problem; requires ambulance + emergency physician |

## Example Indicators  

### Severity 1  
- No circulatory problems reported  
- Patient feels at most mildly unwell  
- Mild blood pressure deviations without vital threat  
- Patient is only slightly dizzy  

### Severity 2  
- Markedly elevated blood pressure without relevant accompanying symptoms  
- Cardiac arrhythmia without acute vital threat  
- Mild angina pectoris symptoms (mild acute chest pain)  
- Patient collapsed/syncope of unclear cause, now awake and stable  
- Brief loss of consciousness due to dizziness/circulatory problems with rapid recovery  

### Severity 3  
- Cardiac arrest or ongoing resuscitation  
- Severe circulatory disorder with acute life-threatening condition (e.g. cold sweating, severely impaired consciousness, barely responsive)  
- Acute onset or progressively severe chest pain, especially with shortness of breath, nausea, cold sweating, or collapse  
- Markedly slow or very rapid pulse with signs of circulatory instability (e.g. syncope, hypotension)  
- Severe external or suspected internal bleeding with signs of shock (e.g. spurting bleeding, massive injury, reduced consciousness, pale cold skin)  
- Severely elevated blood pressure with signs of end-organ damage (e.g. resting dyspnea, acute chest pain, thunderclap headache)  

## Confidence Score  
- Range: **0.0–1.0**  
- High confidence = clear, explicit indicators in the transcript.  
- Low confidence = incomplete, indirect, or contradictory indicators.  
- Incorrect assessment with high confidence results in a high penalty.
