# D – Disability Assessment 

## Context  
Analyze transcribed emergency calls to assess **disability** and neurological status (exclude airway, breathing, circulation).

## Goal  
Identify whether neurological status (consciousness, orientation, speech, motor function, seizures, behavior) is normal, impaired, or critically compromised based on caller descriptions.

## Key Notes  
- Assess only **neurological/mental status**: consciousness, orientation, speech, motor deficits, seizures, vision, behavior.  
- Typical problems: stroke, seizure, head injury, altered mental status, intoxication, acute psychiatric crisis, suicidal intent.  
- Use caller descriptions such as “confused”, “not responding”, “cannot move arm/leg”, “facial droop”, “speaks strangely”, “shaking or jerking”, “threatens suicide”.  
- If evidence is unclear, infer cautiously (e.g. brief loss of consciousness with rapid recovery = at least relevant impairment) and reflect uncertainty in the confidence score.

## Severity Scale  
| Severity | Description                                                                            |
| -------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **0**    | No information available or no conclusion possible                                     |
| **1**    | Normal or minor impairment; no immediate intervention required                         |
| **2**    | Significant impairment; ambulance intervention required                                |
| **3**    | Critical or life-threatening neurological problem; requires ambulance + emergency physician |

## Example Indicators  

### Severity 1  
- No problems reported  
- Patient is responsive and fully oriented  
- Mild headache  
- Mild dizziness  
- Slowly progressive deterioration without recognizable vital threat  

### Severity 2  
- Suspected stroke without acute vital threat  
- Facial or limb paralysis, speech disturbances, newly developed confusion  
- Severe headache without acute loss of consciousness  
- Marked dizziness, but patient responsive  
- Patient is clearly drowsy but can be safely awakened and responds appropriately  
- Patient had a seizure; seizure has ended; patient is recovering  
- Impaired consciousness due to known or suspected hypoglycemia  
- Acute psychological crisis or acute psychosis without current risk to self or others  
- Explicit suicidal ideation or intent, but patient responsive  

### Severity 3  
- Cardiac arrest or ongoing resuscitation  
- Deep unconsciousness; patient not arousable  
- Ongoing seizure or repeated short seizures without full recovery in between (status epilepticus)  
- Persistent unconsciousness after significant head trauma  
- Loss of consciousness preceded by sudden severe “thunderclap” headache  
- Neurological symptoms with acute life-threatening condition (e.g. severe impairment of consciousness, severe breathing or swallowing impairment due to neurological cause)  
- Suicide attempt in progress or recently carried out (e.g. strangulation, jump from height, ingestion of large amounts of medication), unclear or life-threatening condition  

## Confidence Score  
- Range: **0.0–1.0**  
- High confidence = clear, explicit indicators in the transcript.  
- Low confidence = incomplete, indirect, or contradictory indicators.  
- Incorrect assessment with high confidence results in a high penalty.
