# A — Airway

## Severity 1
- No airway problem indicators
- Patient speaks clearly, no distress
- Mild irritation (e.g., common cold) without vital risk

## Severity 2
- Mild airway restriction or minor injury
- Minor allergic reaction without swelling

## Severity 3
- Suddend slurred or “thick” speech; communication impossible
- Rapidly swelling airway
- Chocking with ineffective breathing or inability to speak
- Severe bleeding in mouth/throat affecting breathing
- Airway/ventilation emergency in a tracheostomized patient
- Cardiac arrest / resuscitation


# B — Breathing

## Severity 1
- No breathing issues reported
- Patient speaks clearly in full sentences
- Mild cold or irritation without vital threat

## Severity 2
- Mild shortness of breath (e.g., COPD, asthma) without acute danger
- Hyperventilation due to stress or emotional cause
- Slowly developing dyspnea without vital compromise

## Severity 3
- Severe dyspnea with one- or two-word speech, confusion, or cyanosis
- Exhausted patient unable to hold head upright or remain seated
- Unresponsive or cyanotic patient, no reaction to voice or pain
- Chest pain with dyspnea after chest trauma
- Respiratory arrest or ongoing resuscitation


# C — Circulation

## Severity 1
- No circulatory problems reported
- Patient feels only mildly unwell
- Mild blood pressure deviation without vital threat
- Mild dizziness

## Severity 2
- Markedly elevated blood pressure without relevant symptoms
- Cardiac arrhythmia without acute vital threat
- Mild angina pectoris symptoms
- Unexplained syncope/fall, now awake and stable
- Brief loss of consciousness with rapid recovery
- Injury with moderate blood loss

## Severity 3
- Cardiac arrest or ongoing resuscitation
- Severe circulatory instability with vital threat (cold sweat, severe confusion, barely responsive)
- Sudden or progressive severe chest pain, especially with dyspnea, nausea, diaphoresis, or collapse
- Very slow or very fast pulse with signs of instability (syncope, hypotension)
- Severe external or suspected internal bleeding with shock signs
- Severe hypertension with end-organ symptoms (rest dyspnea, acute chest pain, thunderclap headache)


# D — Disability (Neurological)

## Severity 1
- No problems reported
- Patient alert and fully oriented
- Mild headache
- Mild dizziness
- Slowly progressive deterioration without vital threat

## Severity 2
- Suspected stroke without acute vital threat
- Facial/extremity weakness, speech disturbance, new confusion
- Severe headache without loss of consciousness
- Severe dizziness but patient responsive
- Marked drowsiness but arousable and responsive
- Seizure occurred; now resolved and patient recovering
- Altered consciousness due to suspected hypoglycemia
- Acute psychological crisis or psychosis without immediate danger
- Suicidal ideation but patient responsive

## Severity 3
- Cardiac arrest or ongoing resuscitation
- Deep unconsciousness; patient not arousable
- Ongoing or repeated seizures without full recovery (status epilepticus)
- Persistent unconsciousness after significant head trauma
- Unconsciousness following sudden severe headache
- Neurological symptoms with vital threat (e.g., impaired breathing/swallowing)
- Suicide attempt in progress or recently performed with unstable condition


# E — Exposure / Environment

## Severity 1
- No problems reported
- Minor superficial injuries (small lacerations, abrasions)
- Mild pain without signs of serious injury
- Brief minor exposure (smoke, cold, heat) without symptoms

## Severity 2
- Minor to moderate accidents without signs of life-threatening injury
- Hypothermia without impaired consciousness (shivering, alert)
- Hyperthermia or heat exhaustion without impaired consciousness
- Significant burns in an awake, responsive patient
- Smoke inhalation effects (cough, irritation) without vital threat
- Injuries or bleeding without current life threat
- Moderate to severe pain from isolated extremity trauma without shock signs

## Severity 3
- Severe traffic accident with ejection from vehicle
- Severe crash with major vehicle deformation and entrapment
- Crush injury to torso or head
- Fall >3 m with unconsciousness or severe impairment
- Explosion injury with significant force exposure
- Drowning/ice breakthrough with unconsciousness or severe impairment
- Severe hypothermia with unconscious or barely responsive patient
- Mass casualty incident with multiple severely injured patients
- Extremity injury with deformity, neurological deficit, or absent pulse
- Extreme pain after major trauma, burns, or crush injury with suspected shock
- Iminent childbirth with crowning
- Recent delivery with life-threatening condition of mother or newborn
